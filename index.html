Unordered list "ul"

Unordered list (often called a "bullet list" - but remember, there's no formatting in HTML!) Used for lists where the items have no critical order of execution Always contains "li" tags!

  • Something
  • Another thing
  • Also this one
MDN - Unordered List
********************************************************************************************************************

List item "li"

Always nested inside a "ul" or "ol" tag!

  • Something
  • Another thing
  • Also this one
MDN - List Item
********************************************************************************************************************

Ordered list "ol"

Used for lists where the order is important (like a list of instructions). Often called a "number list" - but remember there's no formatting in HTML! Always contains "li" tags!

  1. Something
  2. Another thing
  3. Also this one

Order List Exercise

Create an ordered list of steps to construct a peanut butter and jelly sandwich.

Make a Peanut Butter and Jelly Sandwich

  1. Get some peanut butter, jelly and 2 pieces of bread
  2. Spread peanut butter on one piece of bread and jelly on the other
  3. Put the peanut butter and jelly together, with the bread side facing out
  4. Eat and enjoy!

Links and anchors "a"

Links can go anywhere: to pages on your site, to pages on other sites, or to files (like a PDF).

Go to Google

Go to Google

Anchors are useful for navigating within a single web page. You need to code an anchor and a link pointing to the anchor to make this work. The anchor is placed where you want the user to start reading. The link is placed where you wish the user to click to skip to that anchor. "Back to top" links are a typical example.

Link to an anchor: Back to top
